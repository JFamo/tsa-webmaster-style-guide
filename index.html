Style Guide?

A style guide helps create a consistent Brand Image.

This style guide was created for Boyertown TSA's 2018-19 webmaster team, based on the original style guide from RIT's BTC club by Ethan Witherington.

Brand Ethos

Purpose

Decentralized Systems: Education and Implementation

Our website will attempt to draw people towards our resources and events.

Resources include past meeting presentations and videos, and eventually our Wiki, which will contain tutorials, explanations, and other content.

Club Audience

College students at RIT.

Our target audience requires a specific tone of voice to have the maximum impact. For more information, see the Tone and Voice section.

We can expect most of our members to have these majors:

  • Computer Science
  • Computing Security
  • Computer Engineering
  • Electrical Engineering
  • Mathematics
  • Economics / Business / Finance

Logo, Brandname

Logo

Currently, for now, temporarily, for the time being, our logo is the BTCRIT Hamster.

SVG files may be scaled to any size. Detail can be lost at small sizes, use an alternative instead.

Appropriate backgrounds include Dark, DarkGray, and Base. Accent and lighter colors provide too little contrast. See the Color Section for more..

Brandname

Long (Official) Form:

Blockchain Technology Club
  • First letter of each word is capitalized
  • RIT does not appear in this form
  • RIT Cannot appear in our official name
  • Stand-Alone, no other words should follow it.
  • Roboto Bold

Short (Informal) Form:

BTCRIT
  • No space, hyphen, or @ symbol
  • All letters capitalized
  • Serves as an Alias for our oficial name
  • May be used informally
  • Roboto Bold
  • May be followed by other specifiers
  • For Example:

Color Palette

Dark: #1e1e1e

DarkGray: #43494D

Gray: #E9EBED

LightGray: #F5F5F5

Light: #FBFBFB

Base: #3e92cc

Accent: #D62828

The main color of the logo was extracted as the Accent color. From there, Base was chosen such that Accent would complement it.

When using these colors, ensure there is enough contrast between backgrounds and text. LightGray is typically the best background, as there are both lighter and darker colors to complement it.
